# Shortlist — Pitch Outline

## Title
Shortlist

## Tagline
From endless options to confident decisions.

## Opening Story
On Wednesday, my mother called me from India and asked: “Can you help me find a vegetable chopper?”

It sounded like a five-minute task.

I opened Amazon. There were hundreds of options.

I watched YouTube videos, including Tamil creators reviewing similar products.

I checked ratings. I compared materials. I looked for dishwasher safety. I thought about whether it would be easy for my parents to use and clean.

I checked again this morning.

And I still have not placed the order.

Not because I could not find products. Because I could not decide.

## Problem
Search solved discovery. It did not solve decision-making.

Amazon gives options. YouTube gives opinions. Reviews give noise. Sponsored rankings and influencer content make trust harder.

What I needed was a shortlist I could trust.

## Product
Shortlist is an AI buying advisor that researches product options against your actual constraints, finds red flags, and gives a clear recommendation.

For this demo, it helps me choose a vegetable chopper for my elderly parents in India.

## Demo Flow
1. I type: “Find a vegetable chopper for my elderly parents in India.”
2. Shortlist loads remembered preferences.
3. It researches options using Exa.
4. It checks evidence and red flags.
5. It evaluates constraints: easy cleaning, dishwasher safe, elderly-friendly, low food-contact plastic, durability.
6. It returns a ranked shortlist.
7. It saves a new preference using Mem0.
8. Manus optionally gives an independent second opinion.

## What Makes It Different
Most shopping tools optimize for products.

Shortlist optimizes for confidence.

It does not just ask “what is popular?”

It asks:
- What matters to this buyer?
- What could go wrong?
- What evidence supports this choice?
- What should we remember for next time?

## Technical Stack
- Vercel AI SDK + AI Gateway for orchestration and model access
- Exa for web research and evidence collection
- Mem0 for persistent decision preferences
- Manus for independent verification
- Next.js for the demo UI

## Close
I started with a simple problem: helping my mother buy a vegetable chopper.

Two days later, I realized the bigger pain was not search. It was trust.

Shortlist turns endless options into confident decisions.

Amazon gives options. Shortlist gives confidence.
